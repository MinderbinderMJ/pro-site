Michael J. Dwyer

Ph.D. Candidate, Supply Chain Management
Debbie and Jerry Ivy College of Business, Iowa State University
Starting Fall 2026: Assistant Professor of Transportation and International Logistics
Texas A&M International University

Michael J. Dwyer is a Ph.D. candidate in Supply Chain Management at the Debbie and Jerry Ivy College of Business, Iowa State University. Beginning Fall 2026, he will join Texas A&M International University as an Assistant Professor of Transportation and International Logistics.

His animating logic is that research should inform what we teach, students engage with best practices and apply them in real-world contexts, those applications surface new challenges for investigation, and those challenges generate new opportunities for scholarly inquiry. This cycle guides how he builds a research agenda that remains relevant while enhancing student learning and improving system-level outcomes.

His research investigates emerging forms of logistics coordination, including crowdsourced delivery and ride-hailing systems, using behavioral methods, agent-based simulation, and qualitative approaches. He is also engaged in ongoing research using econometric modeling to study service system performance. His dissertation, Participation and Platform Design in Crowdsourced Logistics, examines how digitally mediated logistics systems shape the experience, behavior, and outcomes of platform-based workers.

His work spans behavioral experiments, agent-based simulation, qualitative methods, and econometric modeling.

Prior to his doctoral studies, Michael built over fifteen years of professional experience across military, corporate, and nonprofit sectors. He served as a Staff Sergeant and F-15 crew chief in the U.S. Air Force, managed operational and financial risk in insurance and finance, and conducted ethnographic and humanitarian supply chain research with NGOs operating in resource-constrained environments in Guatemala. He is bilingual in English and Spanish. He holds an M.B.A. from the University of West Florida and a B.A. in Philosophy from James Madison University.

In the classroom, he focuses on helping students develop structured, applied understanding of complex systems. He is the recipient of the 2025 Graduate and Professional Student Senate Teaching Award at Iowa State University.
